Research Interests

Operator Theory Function Theory Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces Frames on Hilbert Spaces Sampling Theory and Reconstruction Non-linear Dynamical System Approximation Deep Learning

My research interest spans Dynamical Systems, Operator Theory, Complex Analysis, and Applied Harmonic Analysis. During my Ph.D., my research was mainly focused on characterizing the invariant subspaces of non-trivial operators.

Currently, I am working on the problems of data-driven dynamical systems. I am exploring the idea of embedding the finite-dimensional non-linear dynamics system into infinite-dimensional Hilbert space of continuous functions (RKHS) under the action of linear operators, like the Liouville Operator. This approach facilitates the transformation of non-linear systems into linear systems, offering deeper insight into their behaviour.

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

As a serious mathematician, I have always believed that if learned properly, mathematics is a fascinating subject, and the best way to learn more is to talk about it with other people.

Be Transparent
I provide my students detailed rubrics for assignments and exams, along with annotated examples of high-quality work.
Be Accessible & Respect Time
I want my students to understand that I am investing my time in their education. I support learning beyond the classroom and offer flexible appointments — in-person or virtual.
Show the Bigger Picture
Students need to know why, what, and how in order to be motivated. At the start of each class I provide examples to convey the big picture of the subject matter.
